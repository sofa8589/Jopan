Get inspired for your next trip

Kobe

Kobe is a large cosmopolitan center with thriving restaurants, bars and a vibrant nightlife, not to mention Nanjing Machi — the most famous Chinatown in Japan.

Fukuoka

Fukuoka is a city in Japan in the northern part of the island of Kyushu, is the center of the prefecture of the same name. An industrial city and a major port is divided by the Nakagawa River into two districts – commercial Hakata and business Fukuoka. The metro is functioning.

Kamakura

You can easily reach the six temples from the Kita (North) Kamakura train station, which is about an hour's drive from Tokyo via the Yokosuka line.

Matsuyama

in the island of Shikoku, in the southern side of the Land of the Rising Sun, lies the city of Matsuyama. The oldest onsen, unusual figurines, a real castle, fairy tales and competitions among thousands of poets — the capital of Ehime Prefecture delights not only foreign tourists, but also the residents of Japan themselves

Ise

Ise is a small town located 300 kilometers west of Tokyo. The Ise Jingu temple complex is the most important religious center of the country, a repository of Shinto treasures, and an object of mass pilgrimage.
